Survey (subscribers / mailing list)

In 2023, subscribers were asked: what is the #1 thing stopping you from taking CT Foundation? 54 responded. What they say — and what they don't say — reveals the real conversion problem.

The core finding

The dominant blocker is not opposition, confusion, or price. It is the absence of a felt cost to waiting. Nobody in this dataset experiences a consequence for deferring. This is an activation problem, not a positioning or pricing problem.

Metal fatigue — CT vs WW

If CT needs 5x the email touches that Wholeness Work needs to convert the same audience, the problem isn't the product — it's the story. WW is fresh. CT's message has been in circulation for 30 years. Same infrastructure, same list, different response. That's campaign fatigue, not a positioning problem. The CT story needs a new reason to be told — not louder, but differently.

Referral — the alumni channel

CT alumni — people who have experienced the training — bringing in people who are not on the list at all. How do we set up a referral structure?

Why this is a separate track

Track 1 works on people who already know about CT but haven't acted. Track 2 reaches people who have never heard of CT — but trust someone who has lived it. The message, the mechanic, and the audience are all different. Building one does not build the other.

The parts work lever

Parts work has gone mainstream. IFS grew 30%+ year on year and put "parts" into everyday therapeutic language. CT is parts work — one of only a handful of serious methods in the world — and it predates most of them. But the CT site doesn't use the word "parts" anywhere. This is a new audience waiting to discover CT: people who are already sold on parts work, already doing IFS or similar, and searching for what goes deeper. The referral message from alumni in this space writes itself: "I trained in IFS. CT goes underneath it."

01
The mechanism
CT alumni receive a personal referral link. A friend who registers through it gets a discount on CT Foundation. The alumni get a meaningful reward in return — a mini-course, a Q&A session, something of genuine value. The transaction is tracked in GHL with minimal new infrastructure.
02
Why alumni can do what email can't
The survey shows the #1 blocker for database subscribers is low urgency. Alumni referrals bypass that entirely — they bring in a new person who hasn't been in the CT orbit at all. The conversion dynamic is different: it runs on trust and personal testimony, not on another email from the list.
The message that makes referral work

The survey gave us the exact message. A respondent who had been through NLP training and had CT explained to him wrote this unprompted:

"Emphasize that you need to experience Core Transformation — the theory or a guided example is far less effective."

— Ron Fiedler, survey respondent (NLP practitioner, already familiar with CT)

This is what alumni can say that no promotional email can say. They have the experience. The referral message is not "my friend is offering a discount." It is: "I thought I knew this. I didn't. Here's what changed when I actually did it."

Suggested referral message frames
For alumni who came from the book
"I read the CT book years ago. I thought I had it. The training showed me I had the map — not the territory."
Speaks directly to the largest blocker segment in the survey (19% "already know CT"). Alumni who came from the book can speak to this with authority.
For alumni who came from other modalities
"I've trained in IFS, NLP, somatic work. CT doesn't replace any of it. It goes underneath all of it."
Addresses the training overload cluster — practitioners who feel their practice is already full. Positions CT as depth, not addition.
Universal frame
"I kept thinking I'd get to it. I finally did. I wish I hadn't waited."
Speaks to the low-urgency cluster directly — the people who have been "about to do it" for years. Works for almost any alumni profile.
